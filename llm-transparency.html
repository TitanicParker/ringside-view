AI-use statement

A note on the use of OpenAI’s ChatGPT in preparing, recording, testing, revising, and presenting A1–A6.

ChatGPT was used extensively

A great deal of this work was developed with the help of OpenAI’s ChatGPT. That should be said plainly.

ChatGPT was used as a working instrument: to record, draft, organize, test, revise, and present the work. It helped make documents, build explanations, test examples, compare formulations, generate HTML materials, and return repeatedly to the central question from different angles.

ChatGPT was not the author of the idea, and it is not the authority behind the method.

Where ChatGPT helped and where it failed

The structure I am trying to show sits underneath ordinary meaning. Human beings read sentences linearly. Meaning appears so successfully that the structure which allowed it to appear often vanishes from view. A sentence lands, and once it lands, we tend to live in the meaning rather than inspect the movement that produced it.

Large language models present a different difficulty. They are extraordinarily good at global association, pattern completion, paraphrase, and fluent continuation. But this strength can make them poor witnesses to the precise thing A1–A6 is trying to isolate. The model tends to read globally before it reads procedurally. It tends to move toward semantic familiarity before the underlying sentence-work has been held still.

The central issue is that fluency is not the same as disciplined sentence completion.

Why A1–A6 separates packet formation from resolution

A1–A6 separates the formation of the sentence packet from the later resolution of the axes. It asks first: what was said, what was governed, what work was done, within what range, what residue remains, and what rival reading is excluded? Only after that packet is formed does the method ask for Operator, Orientation, and Remainder.

This separation is a safeguard.

For example, if a sentence says “The door closed” or “The door is closed,” the visible word “closed” can easily pull a reader, or a model, toward the Operator called Closure. But the word “closed” is not enough. The question is not whether the sentence contains the word closure, closed, closing, or close. The question is what work the sentence actually performs, what it governs, what remains, and what rival reading is excluded.

A sentence may contain the vocabulary of closure while landing by Relation, Distinction, Correction, Expansion, Integration, or another dominant Operator. That is why the packet must be formed before the axis is named.

A long correction process

Over roughly ten months, across hundreds of working days, I had to teach ChatGPT the system again and again. The model often had to be brought back from paraphrase into procedure, from global meaning into sentence movement, from fluent classification into disciplined packet formation.

The project was not produced by asking a model for an idea. It was produced through repeated correction, interruption, and testing of model outputs against the procedure.

What the model helped produce

ChatGPT helped externalize the work. It helped draft records, generate corpora, compare examples, build HTML materials, and expose the system to repeated pressure.

But ChatGPT was not the author of the idea, and it is not the authority behind the idea. Its outputs required repeated correction and should not be treated as independent authority.

The model’s outputs required correction before they could support the work.

Read onward

This statement belongs beside the theory and validation material. It explains the role of language-model assistance without replacing authorship, evidence, or inspection.
