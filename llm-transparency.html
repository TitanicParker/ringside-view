A note on the use of OpenAI's ChatGPT in the making, recording, testing, and externalisation of the A1-A6 project.

ChatGPT was used extensively

A great deal of this work was developed with the help of OpenAI's ChatGPT. That should be said plainly.

ChatGPT was used as a working instrument: to record, draft, organise, test, rephrase, externalise, and preserve the structure of an idea that I was trying to bring out of my own head and into public form. It helped me make documents, build explanations, test examples, compare formulations, generate HTML artifacts, and keep returning to the central question from many directions.

But it did not make the work easy. In some ways, it made the work harder.

The difficulty was part of the discovery

The structure I am trying to show sits underneath ordinary meaning. Human beings read sentences linearly. Meaning appears so successfully that the structure which allowed it to appear often vanishes from view. A sentence lands, and once it lands, we tend to live in the meaning rather than inspect the movement that produced it.

Large language models present a different difficulty. They do not read as human beings read. They are extraordinarily good at global association, pattern completion, paraphrase, and fluent continuation. But this very strength can make them poor witnesses to the precise thing A1-A6 is trying to isolate. The model tends to read globally before it reads procedurally. It tends to move toward semantic familiarity before the underlying sentence-work has been held still.

The central discovery was not that a model could produce fluent language. The central discovery was that fluency is not the same as lawful completion.

Why A1-A6 had to separate packet formation from resolution

A1-A6 separates the formation of the sentence packet from the later resolution of the axes. It asks first: what was said, what was governed, what work was done, within what range, what residue remains, and what rival reading is excluded? Only after that packet is formed does the method ask for Operator, Orientation, and Remainder.

This separation is not decorative. It is a safeguard.

For example, if a sentence says "The door closed" or "The door is closed," the visible word "closed" can easily pull a reader, or a model, toward the Operator called Closure. But the word "closed" is not enough. The question is not whether the sentence contains the word closure, closed, closing, or close. The question is what work the sentence actually performs, what it governs, what remains, and what rival reading is excluded.

A sentence may contain the vocabulary of closure while landing by Relation, Distinction, Correction, Expansion, Integration, or another dominant Operator. That is why the packet must be formed before the axis is named.

A long correction process

Over roughly ten months, across hundreds of working days, I had to teach ChatGPT the system again and again. The model often had to be brought back from paraphrase into procedure, from global meaning into sentence movement, from fluent classification into disciplined packet formation.

Only recently have some project-specific contexts become informed enough to work inside the system without beginning from doubt or confusion. That history matters. It means this project was not produced by asking a model for an idea. It was produced through a long struggle to make an idea stable enough that even a powerful language model could be trained, corrected, interrupted, and redirected toward it.

What the model helped produce

ChatGPT helped me externalise the work. It helped me write the record. It helped me generate corpora, compare examples, build HTML artifacts, preserve working versions, and expose the system to repeated pressure. Some byproducts of that process, including the geometry corpus and the recovery studies, now stand as part of the wider archive.

But ChatGPT was not the author of the idea, and it is not the authority behind the idea. It was a demanding instrument, a difficult witness, a recorder, a collaborator under correction, and often a pressure-test.

The work had to be taught to the tool that helped preserve it.

That is part of the story.
