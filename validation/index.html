What these files contain

The validation studies provide detailed evidence behind the A1–A6 validation overview. They are not substitutes for independent validation, but they are serious public materials: structured corpora, locked packet recovery, three-axis resolution, and study-facing interpretation.

The two main study files are available below.

Bounded claim

The studies support procedural seriousness and bounded corpus validity. They show that A1–A6 packets can be completed at scale and that Operator, Orientation, and Remainder can be recovered from locked packets in completed datasets.

They do not prove final universality across every sentence, genre, language, historical period, or oral form. They make the next challenge precise.

What to inspect first

Look first at whether the A1–A6 packet is formed before the axes are named. Then inspect whether the recovered Operator, Orientation, and Remainder are grounded in that packet rather than in a loose paraphrase of sentence meaning.

What remains open

The next stage should include independent readers, blind samples, hostile cases, non-formulaic packet audits, genre expansion, and inter-reader reliability. The studies matter because they make those next tests concrete.
