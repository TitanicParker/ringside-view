Validation

Validation

For readers who want to inspect the procedural, corpus, and study-facing layer of A1–A6 rather than relying on the introductory presentation alone.

What has been tested

A1–A6 is presented with supporting study files, defined procedures, corpus limits, and stated next tests. The studies include source-language packet resolution, axis recovery, cell validation, distribution analysis, translation stability checks, and limitation statements.

The current claim is bounded: the method has been tested on defined corpora, and the next step is independent validation.

What these studies support

These studies support procedural seriousness and bounded corpus validity. They show that A1–A6 packets can be completed at scale. They show that Operator, Orientation, and Remainder can be recovered from locked packets in completed datasets.

They also make the inspection burden public. The reader can move from the introduction into the full study files rather than being asked to accept a condensed claim.

What they do not prove

They do not, by themselves, prove final universality across every sentence, genre, language, historical period, or oral form. Their value is that they make the next challenge precise: independent readers, blind samples, hostile cases, non-formulaic packet audits, genre expansion, and inter-reader reliability.

The validation material is a serious beginning, not a demand that every possible case has already been settled.

How to read the validation material

Begin with the bounded claim. The studies support that the procedure can be completed at scale in the corpora presented, and that the three-axis recovery can be carried through from locked packets.

Then inspect the full study files. The overview is a guide, not a substitute for the underlying evidence.

Next stronger tests

The next validation challenge is precise: independent readers, blind samples, hostile cases, non-formulaic packet audits, genre expansion, oral forms, historical distance, and inter-reader reliability.

The short version

A1–A6 is supported by study files, but it still needs independent testing. Anyone asking “on what grounds is this based?” should begin here, then open the full study files.
