Rigor and validation

For statistically minded readers who want to inspect the procedural, corpus, and validation-facing layer of A1-A6 rather than relying on the introductory presentation alone.

Not a passing intuition

A1-A6 is not being presented as a slogan, a private enthusiasm, or a clever phrase looking for permission. It has already been pushed through structured testing: source-language packet resolution, axis recovery, cell validation, distribution analysis, translation stability checks, and limitation statements.

The claim is still open to challenge. It should be challenged. But it should be challenged at the level at which it now stands: as a developed sentence-resolution instrument with corpora, protocols, axes, recovery studies, statistical summaries, and a clear path toward stronger validation.

What these studies do and do not claim

These studies support procedural seriousness and bounded corpus validity. They show that A1-A6 packets can be completed at scale and that Operator, Orientation, and Remainder can be recovered from locked packets in completed datasets.

They do not, by themselves, prove final universality across every sentence, genre, language, historical period, or oral form. Their value is that they make the next challenge precise: independent readers, blind samples, hostile cases, non-formulaic packet audits, genre expansion, and inter-reader reliability.
