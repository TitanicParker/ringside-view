Validation layer

Rigor and validation

For statistically minded readers who want to inspect the procedural, corpus, and validation-facing layer of A1–A6 rather than relying on the introductory presentation alone.

Not a passing intuition

A1–A6 is not being presented as a slogan, a private enthusiasm, or a clever phrase looking for permission. It has already been pushed through structured testing: source-language packet resolution, axis recovery, cell validation, distribution analysis, translation stability checks, and limitation statements.

The claim is still open to challenge. It should be challenged. But it should be challenged at the level at which it now stands: as a developed sentence-resolution instrument with corpora, protocols, axes, recovery studies, statistical summaries, and a clear path toward stronger validation.

What these studies support

These studies support procedural seriousness and bounded corpus validity. They show that A1–A6 packets can be completed at scale. They show that Operator, Orientation, and Remainder can be recovered from locked packets in completed datasets.

They also make the inspection burden public. The reader can move from the introduction into the full study files rather than being asked to accept a condensed claim.

What they do not prove

They do not, by themselves, prove final universality across every sentence, genre, language, historical period, or oral form. Their value is that they make the next challenge precise: independent readers, blind samples, hostile cases, non-formulaic packet audits, genre expansion, and inter-reader reliability.

This site therefore avoids grandiose certainty. The validation layer is a serious beginning, not a demand that every possible case has already been settled.

How to read the validation layer

Begin with the bounded claim. The studies support that the procedure can be completed at scale in the corpora presented, and that the three-axis recovery can be carried through from locked packets.

Then inspect the full study files. The overview is a doorway, not a substitute for the underlying artifacts.

Next stronger tests

The next validation challenge is precise: independent readers, blind samples, hostile cases, non-formulaic packet audits, genre expansion, oral forms, historical distance, and inter-reader reliability. These are not embarrassments to hide. They are the proper next tests for a sentence-resolution instrument that wants to be taken seriously.

The short version

This project is not arriving with one shoe on and one shoe off. It is the result of a long body of work that has already been formalized, tested, corrected, tabulated, and exposed to statistical pressure. Anyone asking “on what grounds is this based?” should begin here, then open the full study files.
